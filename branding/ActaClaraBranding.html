Outer chevron
Open upward-pointing chevron. No fill. No shaft. No container. Stroke weight: 3pt at canonical size (80×64 viewBox). Apex at top center. Base endpoints equidistant from center axis. Round joins and caps throughout.
Inner chevron
Concentric inner chevron, same apex point as outer. Stroke weight: ~53% of outer. Opacity: ~38% of outer. Creates depth and signal-emergence effect without additional color. The weight+opacity differential is the ownable element.
What it is not
Not a military chevron (those are filled, inverted, or enclosed). Not a caret (^) — different proportions and stroke treatment. Not a circumflex — different context and geometry. No prior form in Unicode or common typographic usage matches this specific two-weight nested configuration.

VII — Trademark Summary for Counsel
Mark description · distinguishing features · filing recommendation
Mark description (USPTO)
The mark consists of two concentric open upward-pointing chevrons, no fill, no enclosing container, no shaft. The outer chevron is rendered at full stroke weight. The inner chevron is rendered at approximately 50% stroke weight and 38% opacity of the outer, creating a visual hierarchy of signal over echo. Round stroke joins and terminals throughout. All geometry is custom — no Unicode or standard typographic basis.
Distinguishing features
(1) Two-chevron nested configuration — not one. (2) Differential weight + opacity between outer and inner — the combination of both variables is the ownable element. (3) Absence of shaft, container, or fill — distinguishes from arrow marks, military chevrons, and caret symbols. (4) Proportions: canonical apex ~52°, inner chevron inset approximately 30% from outer base endpoints.
Recommended filing
Design mark — standard character mark separately for wordmark "ACTACLARA." Priority classes: 42 (data intelligence software, SaaS), 35 (business intelligence, research services), 36 (financial analysis and intelligence services). Request design search in all three classes before filing. Particular attention to Class 35 — highest density of existing chevron-adjacent marks in intelligence/analytics space.
